02 — Evolution

My Tech Journey

Most engineers pick a lane and stay there. I've deliberately jumped across worlds — from game engines to spatial computing to native Android to cross-platform SDKs. Each leap wasn't a detour; it was compound interest on curiosity.

2021 – 2022Game Development

Started where imagination meets interactivity

My first serious engineering work was in game development — a domain that demands real-time systems, physics, rendering pipelines, and player psychology all at once. Building a zombie survival AR game for Jio Glasses at Shortgun taught me performance budgets, frame timing, and how to ship something that has to feel good — not just work correctly.

UnityC#Jio SDKGame PhysicsXR Hardware
2022AR / VR

Went deeper into spatial computing

I didn't stop at games — I pushed into full augmented and virtual reality. At CSKAA, I built a Meta University virtual education environment and a photorealistic 3D Car Visualizer. This era sharpened my understanding of 3D rendering, scene graphs, and how humans interact with space. The best technology disappears — the interface becomes the experience.

Unreal EngineMeta VR SDK3D RenderingScene Design
2021 – 2023Native Android

Grounded in the platform that runs the world

Parallel to game and AR work, I went deep into native Android — starting with Huawei HMS Core at my internship, shipping a fitness app end-to-end on AppGallery. Then at Hughes Systique, I scaled up: architecting MVVM + Coroutines + Flows across 4 production apps, lifting Yatra.com's crash-free sessions to 99.4%+, and delivering 60fps Compose UIs for 100k+ active users. Android became my deepest craft.

2023 – 2024Cross-Platform

Bridged native precision with platform reach

A natural Android engineer could have stopped there. I didn't. I learned React Native to understand how product teams build across iOS and Android simultaneously — and immediately put it to use by building a React Native wrapper around a native voice SDK, cutting partner integration time by 40%. Knowing both worlds lets me make smarter architectural choices.

2024 – PresentSDK & AI

Now building the tools other engineers build on

The current frontier: SDK architecture and AI integration. I've shipped an AI-powered voice recognition SDK, a barcode scanner SDK for industrial hardware, and automated CI pipelines that cut QA cycles by 30%. Every chapter — games, AR, Android, cross-platform — fed into this. I understand latency, device constraints, and production-grade reliability from every angle. What's next?
